Tripe Mishqui

Description

 Beef small intestine, which may be grilled or fried. It is consumed in many Latin American countries.[1]

 In some countries, Tripe Mishqui is grilled over wood or charcoal. In Colombia, Argentina, Paraguay, Uruguay, Peru, Chile and Ecuador, however, it is usually roasted. Despite its long cooking requirement, it is usually the first dish served in a Paraguayan, Uruguayan, or Argentinian asado.

Ingredients

  • 250g Beef intestine
  • 3 green bananas
  • 2 sour oranges
  • 2 cloves of garlic (Chopped)
  • 2 spoonfulls of whole wheat flour
  • 2 branch of Yerba Buena
  • 2 cilantro branch (chopped)
  • 1 red onion (Julienne )
  • 1 tomato
  • 1 lime
  • salt pepper and cumin

Steps

  1. Wash the intestines very well by passing water to jets through it, put in a bowl with the juice of the sour oranges, the two tablespoons of flour and the chopped yerba buena, cover with film paper and let stand for an hour.
  2. Peel and cook the green bananas
  3. Season the intestines with the chopped garlic, pepper and salt
  4. Prepare the Encurtido by combining the onions, tomato, cilantro, salt, and lime
  5. Light the charcoal in the grill and brown the intestines for about 10 minutes or until juices begin flowing from their interior
  6. Cut the untestines and the banana into bite sized pieces
  7. Serve hot with the Encurtido
