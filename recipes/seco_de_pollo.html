Seco De Pollo

Description

This Ecuadorian classic is a stew of chicken, beer, naranjilla, tomatoes, aromatics and spices that tastes just like childhood.

Ingredients

  • 10 - 12 assorted chicken pieces (drumsticks, thighs, breasts)
  • 2 tsp Sazon
  • 2 tsp Adobo
  • 2 tsp Cumin
  • 3-4 tsp achiote oil
  • 1 white onion, cut into 1-inch chunks
  • 6-8 cloves of garlic
  • 5-6 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 red or green bell pepper, seeds removed and cut into 1-inche pieces
  • 1 bunch cilantro
  • 1 cup beer (or chicken brother, white wine, or chicha)
  • 1 cup naranjilla (juice of naranja agria), lulo juice, or fresh orange juice
  • 2 tsp oregano
  • 2 tbsp sofrito
  • 1 jalapeno or serrano pepper (optional)
  • Salt and Pepper to taste

Steps

Prepare the Chicken

  1. Wash your chicken in a mixture of water and either vinegar or lime juice. After cleaning, remove any excess fat.
  2. Pat your chicken dry and use a knife to create slits into the chicken to better absorb the marinade.
  3. In a larger bowl, add your chicken along with 1 tsp achiote oil, Sazon, Adobo, cumim, salt and pepper and mix. Set aside while you prepare the sauce.

Prepare the Sauce

  1. Add the onion, garlic cloves, tomatoes, bell pepper, cilantro (reserving a few springs for garnish) to a blender. If you are using a jalapeno or serrano pepper, add this as well. Blend until smooth.

Cook the Stew

  1. In a large pot or caldero, heat the remaining 2 tsp of achiote oil. Add the chicken pieces and brown them on both sides, about 2 minutes each side.
  2. Once the chicken is browned, pour the blended sauce over the chicken. Then, add the sofrito, beer, oregano, and salt and pepper to taste. Gently mix, then bring to a boil.
  3. Once boiling, reduce the heat to a simmer, cover, and cook on low for about 45 minutes to 1 hours, or until chicken is cooked through and tender.
  4. Taste the sauce and adjust for seasoning.
  5. Serve with your choice of sides, such as arroz amarillo, maduros, avocado slices, and curtido de tomate y cebolla. Enjoy!
